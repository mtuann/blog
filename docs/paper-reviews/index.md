# Paper Reviews

This page tracks deep reads of individual papers. A review here should do more than summarize: it should explain the question, identify the real contribution, note what feels convincing, and surface what still seems fragile or underexplained.

## Review Rubric

Every review should try to answer:

1. What problem is the paper actually solving?
2. What is the core idea in plain language?
3. Why does it matter for models, systems, or evaluation?
4. What evidence is strong, and what evidence is missing?
5. What should an engineer or researcher do differently after reading it?

## Review Categories

- **Model ideas** for architecture and training changes.
- **Systems papers** for serving, scheduling, memory, and hardware efficiency.
- **Trustworthiness papers** for evaluation, robustness, and interpretability.
- **Practical papers** that change how experiments should be run.

## Near-Term Goal

The first wave of reviews should focus on papers with clear downstream value: efficient inference, post-training, evaluation, and systems design. That keeps the archive opinionated instead of broad and shallow.
