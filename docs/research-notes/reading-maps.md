# Reading Maps

Reading maps are meant to answer a practical question: if someone wants to understand a topic from basic ideas to current research, what should they read and in what order?

## What A Good Reading Map Includes

- A one-paragraph mental model of the topic.
- Foundational resources and prerequisites.
- A small set of core papers.
- Frontier directions and unresolved questions.
- Links to internal notes, code deep dives, or system implications.

## Candidate Maps

- Transformer efficiency from attention basics to kernel-aware inference.
- Interpretability from probes to sparse autoencoders.
- Distributed training from DDP to 3D parallelism.
